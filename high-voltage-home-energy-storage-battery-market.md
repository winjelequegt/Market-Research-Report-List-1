<p><p>The high voltage home energy storage battery market features three primary types: Lead Acid, Lithium-Ion, and Sodium-Sulfur. Lead Acid batteries are cost-effective and widely used, but have shorter life spans and lower energy density. Lithium-Ion batteries offer higher efficiency, longer life cycles, and compact designs, making them the most popular choice for home storage. Sodium-Sulfur batteries, operating at high temperatures, provide high energy density and longer durability, but face limitations in installation and energy efficiency, making them less common for residential use.</p></p>
