<p><ul><li>Analog</li><li>Digital</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Mammography imaging is primarily categorized into two types: analog and digital. Analog mammography uses traditional film to capture images of breast tissue, relying on chemical processing for development. In contrast, digital mammography employs electronic detectors to produce high-resolution images that can be easily manipulated, stored, and analyzed. Digital systems offer enhanced image quality, faster processing times, and the ability to perform tomosynthesis, enabling better detection of abnormalities. Both types play crucial roles in breast cancer screening and diagnosis.</p></p>
